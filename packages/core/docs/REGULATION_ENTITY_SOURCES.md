# Regulation entity types – legal and official sources

This document describes how the **entity types** for each built-in regulation are determined and which legal or official texts they align with. Entity types drive which PII the sanitizer detects when a regulation is active.

---

## GDPR – General Data Protection Regulation (EU 2016/679)

- **Legal basis:** Regulation (EU) 2016/679 (EUR-Lex), **Article 4(1)** (definition of personal data), **Article 4(13)–(15)** (genetic, biometric, health data), **Article 9(1)** (special categories), **Recital 30** (online identifiers).
- **Definition (Art. 4(1) – exact):** “Personal data” means any information relating to an identified or identifiable natural person (“data subject”); an identifiable natural person is one who can be identified, directly or indirectly, in particular by reference to an identifier such as **a name, an identification number, location data, an online identifier** or to **one or more factors specific to the physical, physiological, genetic, mental, economic, cultural or social identity** of that natural person.
- **Special categories (Art. 9(1) – exact):** Processing of personal data revealing **racial or ethnic origin, political opinions, religious or philosophical beliefs, or trade union membership**, and the processing of **genetic data, biometric data** for the purpose of uniquely identifying a natural person, **data concerning health** or **data concerning a natural person’s sex life or sexual orientation** shall be prohibited (unless an exception in Art. 9(2) applies).
- **Recital 30:** Natural persons may be associated with online identifiers provided by their devices, applications, tools and protocols, such as **IP addresses, cookie identifiers** or **other identifiers** (e.g. RFID). This may leave traces which, combined with unique identifiers and other information received by the servers, may be used to create profiles and identify individuals.
- **Entity-type mapping:** Name → `PERSON_NAME`, `FIRST_NAME`, `LAST_NAME`; identification number → `NATIONAL_ID`, `PASSPORT_NUMBER`, `TAX_ID`; location → `POSTAL_ADDRESS`, `STREET_ADDRESS`, `CITY`, `LOCATION`, `COORDINATES`; online identifier → `IP_ADDRESS`, `MAC_ADDRESS`, `COOKIE_ID`, `DEVICE_ID`; physical/physiological/health → `BIOMETRIC_ID`, `HEALTH_INSURANCE_ID`, `DIAGNOSIS`, `MEDICATION`, `CLINICAL_NOTE`, `DATE_OF_BIRTH`; economic → `FINANCIAL_ACCOUNT`, `CREDIT_CARD_NUMBER`, `BANK_ACCOUNT_NUMBER`; cultural/social/special → `ETHNICITY`, `POLITICAL_OPINION`, `RELIGION`, `SEXUAL_ORIENTATION`.

**References:** [EUR-Lex 2016/679](https://eur-lex.europa.eu/eli/reg/2016/679/oj), [GDPR Article 4](https://gdpr.eu.org/art/4/), [Article 9](https://gdpr.eu.org/art/9/).

## HIPAA – Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act (USA)

- **Legal basis:** HIPAA Privacy Rule, **45 CFR Part 164, Subpart E**; Safe Harbor de-identification requires removal of **18 identifiers** (HHS Guidance on De-identification of PHI).
- **18 PHI identifiers (summary):** (1) Names; (2) Geographic data smaller than state (street, city, county, zip, geocodes); (3) Dates (birth, admission, discharge, death, and ages over 89); (4) Telephone numbers; (5) Fax numbers; (6) Email; (7) SSN; (8) Medical record numbers; (9) Health plan beneficiary numbers; (10) Account numbers; (11) Certificate/license numbers; (12) Vehicle identifiers and license plates; (13) Device identifiers and serial numbers; (14) Web URLs; (15) IP addresses; (16) Biometric identifiers; (17) Full-face photos; (18) Any other unique identifying number, characteristic, or code.
- **Entity-type mapping:** Names → `PERSON_NAME`; dates → `DATE_OF_BIRTH`; contact → `PHONE_NUMBER`, `EMAIL_ADDRESS`; geographic → `POSTAL_ADDRESS`, `CITY`; MRN → `MEDICAL_RECORD_NUMBER`; health plan → `HEALTH_INSURANCE_ID`; clinical → `DIAGNOSIS`, `MEDICATION`, `CLINICAL_NOTE`; SSN → `SOCIAL_SECURITY_NUMBER`; vehicle → `LICENSE_PLATE`; device → `DEVICE_ID`; online → `IP_ADDRESS`, `URL`; biometric → `BIOMETRIC_ID`.

**References:** [HHS HIPAA](https://www.hhs.gov/hipaa/index.html), [18 PHI Identifiers (Berkeley)](https://cphs.berkeley.edu/hipaa/hipaa18.html), [HHS De-identification Guidance](https://www.hhs.gov/sites/default/files/ocr/privacy/hipaa/understanding/coveredentities/De-identification/hhs_deid_guidance.pdf).

## KVKK – Kişisel Verilerin Korunması Kanunu (6698, Turkey)

- **Legal basis:** 6698 sayılı Kişisel Verilerin Korunması Kanunu (KVKK); Resmî Gazete 7 Nisan 2016, Sayı: 29677. Kişisel Verileri Koruma Kurumu (KVKK) rehberleri ve “Örneklerle Kişisel Verilerin Korunması” dokümanı.
- **Definition (Madde 3(d) – exact):** “Kişisel veri: **Kimliği belirli veya belirlenebilir gerçek kişiye ilişkin her türlü bilgiyi** ifade eder.” Tanım kapsamındaki bilgiler, kişinin fiziksel, ekonomik, kültürel, sosyal veya psikolojik kimliğini ifade eden veya kimlik/vergi/sigorta numarası gibi bir kayıtla ilişkilendirilerek kişiyi belirleyen bilgileri kapsar.
- **Kurum örnekleri (kişisel veri):** Ad, soyad, doğum tarihi, doğum yeri; fiziki, ailevi, ekonomik ve diğer özelliklere ilişkin bilgiler; isim, telefon numarası, motorlu taşıt plakası; sosyal güvenlik numarası, pasaport numarası; rehberde ayrıca: ana adı, baba adı, adres, SGK.
- **Özel nitelikli kişisel veriler (Madde 6 – sınırlı sayıda, kıyasla genişletilemez):** (1) Irk ve etnik köken, (2) Siyasi düşünce, (3) Felsefi inanç, (4) Din, mezhep veya diğer inançlar, (5) Kılık ve kıyafet, (6) Dernek, vakıf ya da sendika üyeliği, (7) Sağlık verileri, (8) Cinsel hayat verileri, (9) Ceza mahkûmiyeti ve güvenlik tedbirleriyle ilgili veriler, (10) Biyometrik veriler, (11) Genetik veriler.
- **Entity-type mapping:** Kimlik/iletişim → `PERSON_NAME`, `FIRST_NAME`, `LAST_NAME`, `NATIONAL_ID`, `PASSPORT_NUMBER`, `SOCIAL_SECURITY_NUMBER`, `TAX_ID`, `PHONE_NUMBER`, `EMAIL_ADDRESS`; adres/konum → `POSTAL_ADDRESS`, `STREET_ADDRESS`, `CITY`, `LOCATION`, `COORDINATES`; tarih/araç → `DATE_OF_BIRTH`, `LICENSE_PLATE`; çevrimiçi tanımlayıcı → `IP_ADDRESS`, `COOKIE_ID`, `DEVICE_ID`; özel nitelikli → `BIOMETRIC_ID`, `DNA_PROFILE`, `HEALTH_INSURANCE_ID`, `DIAGNOSIS`, `MEDICATION`, `CLINICAL_NOTE`, `RELIGION`, `ETHNICITY`, `POLITICAL_OPINION`, `SEXUAL_ORIENTATION`; ekonomik → `FINANCIAL_ACCOUNT`, `CREDIT_CARD_NUMBER`, `BANK_ACCOUNT_NUMBER`; müşteri/üyelik referansları → `CUSTOMER_REFERENCE_ID` (KVKK Md. 3 kişisel veri tanımı bir kişiyi belirli kılan her veriyi kapsar; başvuru formlarındaki müşteri/üye/CRM numaraları bu kapsama girer).

**References:** [kvkk.gov.tr](https://www.kvkk.gov.tr/), [Kişisel Veri / Özel Nitelikli Kişisel Veri (Kurum)](https://www.kvkk.gov.tr/Icerik/6605/Personal-Data-Special-Categories-of-Personal-Data), [Örneklerle Kişisel Verilerin Korunması](https://www.kvkk.gov.tr/Icerik/5521/Orneklerle-Kisisel-Verilerin-Korunmasi-Dokumani-Kurum-Internet-Sayfasinda-Yayinlanmistir-), [Rehberler](https://www.kvkk.gov.tr/Icerik/2030/rehberler).

## LGPD – Lei Geral de Proteção de Dados (Brazil, Law 13.709/2018)

- **Legal basis:** Lei nº 13.709/2018 (LGPD); ANPD guidance.
- **Definition:** Personal data: any information relating to an identified or identifiable natural person. **Sensitive personal data** (Art. 5 II): racial or ethnic origin, religious belief, political opinion, union membership, genetic/biometric data, health, sex life.
- **Examples (dados pessoais):** name, date/place of birth, RG/CPF, photo, address, email, card numbers, income, payment history, location, IP, cookies, phone.
- **Entity-type mapping:** Names → `PERSON_NAME`; IDs → `CPF`, `NATIONAL_ID`; contact → `EMAIL_ADDRESS`, `PHONE_NUMBER`, `POSTAL_ADDRESS`; dates → `DATE_OF_BIRTH`; location → `LOCATION`; online → `IP_ADDRESS`, `COOKIE_ID`; financial → `CREDIT_CARD_NUMBER`, `BANK_ACCOUNT_NUMBER`; health → `HEALTH_INSURANCE_ID`, `DIAGNOSIS`; sensitive → `BIOMETRIC_ID`, `POLITICAL_OPINION`, `RELIGION`, `ETHNICITY`, `SEXUAL_ORIENTATION`.

**References:** [Gov.br LGPD](https://www.gov.br/escola-national-de-administracao-publica/lgpd), [ANPD](https://www.gov.br/anpd/pt-br).

## PIPEDA – Personal Information Protection and Electronic Documents Act (Canada)

- **Legal basis:** PIPEDA, **section 2(1)** (definition of personal information); Office of the Privacy Commissioner (OPC) interpretation bulletins.
- **Definition (s. 2(1) – exact):** “Personal information” means **information about an identifiable individual**.
- **OPC guidance:** Identifiability = serious possibility that an individual could be identified (alone or in combination). Examples: financial/credit history, opinions about the individual, photographs, fingerprints, voice prints, blood type, video/audio where the individual appears or is heard.
- **Entity-type mapping:** Identifiers → `PERSON_NAME`, `FIRST_NAME`, `LAST_NAME`, `EMAIL_ADDRESS`, `PHONE_NUMBER`, `POSTAL_ADDRESS`, `STREET_ADDRESS`, `CITY`, `DATE_OF_BIRTH`, `NATIONAL_ID`; financial → `FINANCIAL_ACCOUNT`, `CREDIT_CARD_NUMBER`, `BANK_ACCOUNT_NUMBER`, `TAX_ID`; health → `HEALTH_INSURANCE_ID`, `DIAGNOSIS`, `MEDICATION`; online → `IP_ADDRESS`, `COOKIE_ID`, `DEVICE_ID`, `LOCATION`; biometric/sensitive → `BIOMETRIC_ID`, `RELIGION`, `ETHNICITY`.

**References:** [PIPEDA s. 2](https://laws-lois.justice.gc.ca/eng/acts/P-8.6/section-2.html), [OPC PIPEDA](https://www.priv.gc.ca/en/privacy-topics/privacy-laws-in-canada/the-personal-information-protection-and-electronic-documents-act-pipeda/).

## CCPA / CPRA – California Consumer Privacy Act (USA, California)

- **Legal basis:** Cal. Civ. Code § **1798.140** (definitions); California Privacy Protection Agency (CPPA). CPRA amends CCPA and adds “sensitive personal information.”
- **Definition (§ 1798.140(o)(1)):** “Personal information” means information that identifies, relates to, describes, is reasonably capable of being associated with, or could reasonably be linked, directly or indirectly, with a particular consumer or household.
- **Categories (from statute):** Identifiers (name, alias, postal address, unique/online identifier, IP, email, SSN, driver’s license, passport, etc.); customer records (name, signature, SSN, address, phone, insurance, bank/card, medical); protected classifications (race, religion, sexual orientation, gender, age); biometric; internet/network activity; geolocation; sensitive PI (SSN, financial account, precise geolocation, racial/ethnic origin, religious beliefs, genetic, health, sexual orientation).

**References:** [California Civil Code 1798.140](https://leginfo.legislature.ca.gov/faces/codes_displaySection.xhtml?lawCode=CIV&sectionNum=1798.140), [CPPA](https://cppa.ca.gov/), [oag.ca.gov/privacy/ccpa](https://oag.ca.gov/privacy/ccpa).

## uk_gdpr – UK General Data Protection Regulation (United Kingdom)

- **Legal basis:** UK GDPR (retained EU Regulation 2016/679 as amended by the Data Protection, Privacy and Electronic Communications (Amendments etc) (EU Exit) Regulations 2019) + Data Protection Act 2018 (DPA 2018). **Article 4(1)** (definition of personal data), **Article 4(13)–(15)** (genetic, biometric, health data definitions), **Article 9(1)** (special categories of personal data). DPA 2018 **Section 3(2)–(4)** (supplementary personal data definition), **Section 10** (criminal offence data processing conditions), **Schedule 1** (conditions for special category and criminal offence data).
- **Definition (Art. 4(1) – paraphrased):** “Personal data” means any information relating to an identified or identifiable living individual (“data subject”); an identifiable individual is one who can be identified, directly or indirectly, in particular by reference to an identifier such as a name, an identification number, location data, an online identifier, or to one or more factors specific to the physical, physiological, genetic, mental, economic, cultural or social identity of that individual. The DPA 2018 Section 3(2) mirrors this definition.
- **Special categories (Art. 9(1)):** Processing of personal data revealing **racial or ethnic origin**, **political opinions**, **religious or philosophical beliefs**, or **trade union membership**, and the processing of **genetic data**, **biometric data** for the purpose of uniquely identifying a natural person, **data concerning health**, or data concerning a natural person's **sex life or sexual orientation** shall be prohibited (unless an Art. 9(2) condition applies). DPA 2018 Section 10 additionally governs **criminal conviction and offence data** processing.
- **Entity-type mapping:** Name → `PERSON_NAME`, `FIRST_NAME`, `LAST_NAME`; identification number → `NATIONAL_ID`, `PASSPORT_NUMBER`, `TAX_ID`; contact → `EMAIL_ADDRESS`, `PHONE_NUMBER`; location → `POSTAL_ADDRESS`, `STREET_ADDRESS`, `CITY`, `LOCATION`, `COORDINATES`; online identifier → `IP_ADDRESS`, `MAC_ADDRESS`, `COOKIE_ID`, `DEVICE_ID`; dates → `DATE_OF_BIRTH`; physical/physiological/health → `BIOMETRIC_ID`, `HEALTH_INSURANCE_ID`, `DIAGNOSIS`, `MEDICATION`, `CLINICAL_NOTE`; economic → `FINANCIAL_ACCOUNT`, `CREDIT_CARD_NUMBER`, `BANK_ACCOUNT_NUMBER`; special categories → `POLITICAL_OPINION`, `RELIGION`, `ETHNICITY`, `SEXUAL_ORIENTATION`.

**References:** [UK GDPR (legislation.gov.uk)](https://www.legislation.gov.uk/eur/2016/679/contents), [DPA 2018 (legislation.gov.uk)](https://www.legislation.gov.uk/ukpga/2018/12/contents), [ICO: What is personal data?](https://ico.org.uk/for-organisations/uk-gdpr-guidance-and-resources/personal-information-what-is-it/what-is-personal-data/what-is-personal-data/), [ICO: What is special category data?](https://ico.org.uk/for-organisations/uk-gdpr-guidance-and-resources/lawful-basis/special-category-data/what-is-special-category-data/), [UK GDPR Article 4 (Fieldfisher)](https://ukgdpr.fieldfisher.com/chapter-1/article-4-gdpr/).

## pdpl_sa – Personal Data Protection Law (Saudi Arabia)

- **Legal basis:** Personal Data Protection Law (PDPL), issued by Royal Decree No. M/19, dated 9 Safar 1443 AH (16 September 2021), amended by Royal Decree No. M/148, dated 5 Sha'ban 1444 AH (27 March 2023). **Article 1** (definitions, including personal data and sensitive data), **Article 10** (data collection conditions), **Article 14** (sensitive data processing conditions). Supervised by the Saudi Data & Artificial Intelligence Authority (SDAIA).
- **Definition (Art. 1 – paraphrased):** “Personal data” means every datum — whatever its source or form — that leads to knowing the individual specifically, or makes it possible to identify them directly or indirectly, including name, personal identification number, addresses, contact numbers, license numbers, personal records, possessions, bank and credit card account numbers, still or moving images of the individual, and other data of a personal nature.
- **Sensitive data (Art. 1 – exhaustive list):** Personal data revealing (1) **racial or ethnic origin**, (2) **religious, intellectual, or political beliefs**, (3) data relating to **criminal convictions and offences**, (4) **biometric data** for the purpose of identifying the person, (5) **genetic data** for the purpose of identifying the person, (6) **health data**, and (7) data that indicates that **one or both of the individual's parents are unknown**.
- **Entity-type mapping:** Name/identification → `PERSON_NAME`, `NATIONAL_ID`, `PASSPORT_NUMBER`; contact → `EMAIL_ADDRESS`, `PHONE_NUMBER`; address/location → `POSTAL_ADDRESS`, `LOCATION`; dates → `DATE_OF_BIRTH`; financial → `FINANCIAL_ACCOUNT`, `CREDIT_CARD_NUMBER`, `BANK_ACCOUNT_NUMBER`; online identifiers → `IP_ADDRESS`, `COOKIE_ID`, `DEVICE_ID`; sensitive → `BIOMETRIC_ID`, `HEALTH_INSURANCE_ID`, `DIAGNOSIS`, `RELIGION`, `ETHNICITY`, `POLITICAL_OPINION`.

**References:** [SDAIA](https://sdaia.gov.sa/), [SDAIA PDPL Guide (English PDF)](https://dgp.sdaia.gov.sa/wps/wcm/connect/f579bc32-fda8-47bd-bc6f-66b8cb77985c/ENG-Guide+to+the+saudi+PDP+law+for+controllersprocessors.pdf?MOD=AJPERES), [SDAIA Executive Regulations (English PDF)](https://sdaia.gov.sa/en/SDAIA/about/Documents/ExecutiveRegulationsEn.pdf), [Saudi PDPL Explained (saudiprivacylaw.com)](https://saudiprivacylaw.com/), [DLA Piper: Saudi Arabia](https://www.dlapiperdataprotection.com/?c=SA).

## nzpa – Privacy Act 2020 (New Zealand)

- **Legal basis:** Privacy Act 2020 (No. 31), commenced 1 December 2020, replacing the Privacy Act 1993. **Section 7** (interpretation, definition of personal information). The Act contains **13 Information Privacy Principles (IPPs)** in Part 3 governing collection, storage, access, correction, use, and disclosure. Supervised by the Office of the Privacy Commissioner.
- **Definition (s. 7 – exact):** “Personal information” means **information about an identifiable individual**. The Privacy Commissioner's guidance clarifies: the definition is deliberately broad; information need not name the individual — they can be identifiable through other means such as address, email, photographs, recordings, or any combination of data points that could identify a specific person.
- **Sensitive/special categories:** The Privacy Act 2020 does **not** prescribe fixed statutory categories of “sensitive” personal information (unlike GDPR or Australia's Privacy Act). However, the Privacy Commissioner's guidance recognises that certain types of information are **inherently sensitive** and require a higher standard of care: health information, genetic and biometric data, financial information, racial or ethnic origin, gender, sexual orientation, religious or cultural beliefs, political beliefs, disability, and mental health history. The Health Information Privacy Code 2020 (issued under s. 32) provides additional rules specifically for health information.
- **Entity-type mapping:** Name/identification → `PERSON_NAME`, `NATIONAL_ID`; contact → `EMAIL_ADDRESS`, `PHONE_NUMBER`; address/location → `POSTAL_ADDRESS`, `LOCATION`; dates → `DATE_OF_BIRTH`; financial → `FINANCIAL_ACCOUNT`, `CREDIT_CARD_NUMBER`, `BANK_ACCOUNT_NUMBER`; health → `HEALTH_INSURANCE_ID`, `DIAGNOSIS`; online identifiers → `IP_ADDRESS`, `COOKIE_ID`, `DEVICE_ID`; biometric → `BIOMETRIC_ID`.

**References:** [Privacy Act 2020 s. 7 (legislation.govt.nz)](https://www.legislation.govt.nz/act/public/2020/0031/latest/LMS23312.html), [Privacy Act 2020 full text (legislation.govt.nz)](https://www.legislation.govt.nz/act/public/2020/0031/latest/whole.html), [Office of the Privacy Commissioner](https://www.privacy.org.nz/), [Privacy Commissioner: Working with sensitive information](https://www.privacy.org.nz/responsibilities/working-with-sensitive-information/), [Sensitive Personal Information and the Privacy Act 2020 (OPC PDF)](https://www.privacy.org.nz/assets/New-order/Your-responsibilities/Privacy-resources-for-organisations/Sensitive-Personal-Information-and-the-Privacy-Act-2020.pdf).

## australia_pa – Privacy Act 1988 (Australia)

- **Legal basis:** Privacy Act 1988 (Cth), as amended (including the Privacy Legislation Amendment (Enforcement and Other Measures) Act 2022). **Section 6(1)** (definition of personal information), **Section 6(1) “sensitive information”** (definition of sensitive information). The Act includes 13 **Australian Privacy Principles (APPs)** in Schedule 1. Supervised by the Office of the Australian Information Commissioner (OAIC).
- **Definition (s. 6(1) – exact):** “Personal information” means **information or an opinion about an identified individual, or an individual who is reasonably identifiable**: (a) whether the information or opinion is true or not; and (b) whether the information or opinion is recorded in a material form or not.
- **Sensitive information (s. 6(1) – exhaustive list):** “Sensitive information” means: (a) information or an opinion about an individual's: (i) **racial or ethnic origin**; (ii) **political opinions**; (iii) **membership of a political association**; (iv) **religious beliefs or affiliations**; (v) **philosophical beliefs**; (vi) **membership of a professional or trade association**; (vii) **membership of a trade union**; (viii) **sexual orientation or practices**; (ix) **criminal record** — that is also personal information; or (b) **health information** about an individual; or (c) **genetic information** about an individual that is not otherwise health information; or (d) **biometric information** that is to be used for the purpose of automated biometric verification or biometric identification; or (e) **biometric templates**.
- **Entity-type mapping:** Name/identification → `PERSON_NAME`, `NATIONAL_ID`; contact → `EMAIL_ADDRESS`, `PHONE_NUMBER`; address/location → `POSTAL_ADDRESS`, `LOCATION`; dates → `DATE_OF_BIRTH`; financial → `FINANCIAL_ACCOUNT`, `CREDIT_CARD_NUMBER`, `BANK_ACCOUNT_NUMBER`; health → `HEALTH_INSURANCE_ID`, `DIAGNOSIS`; online identifiers → `IP_ADDRESS`, `COOKIE_ID`, `DEVICE_ID`; sensitive → `BIOMETRIC_ID`, `RELIGION`, `ETHNICITY`, `POLITICAL_OPINION`, `SEXUAL_ORIENTATION`.

**References:** [Privacy Act 1988 (Federal Register of Legislation)](https://www.legislation.gov.au/C2004A03712/latest), [Privacy Act 1988 s. 6 (AustLII)](https://classic.austlii.edu.au/au/legis/cth/consol_act/pa1988108/s6.html), [OAIC: What is personal information?](https://www.oaic.gov.au/privacy/privacy-guidance-for-organisations-and-government-agencies/handling-personal-information/what-is-personal-information), [OAIC](https://www.oaic.gov.au/privacy/), [ALRC: Sensitive information](https://www.alrc.gov.au/publication/for-your-information-australian-privacy-law-and-practice-alrc-report-108/6-the-privacy-act-some-important-definitions/sensitive-information/).

## pdpa_th – Personal Data Protection Act B.E. 2562 (Thailand)

- **Legal basis:** Personal Data Protection Act B.E. 2562 (2019), published in the Government Gazette Vol. 136, Chapter 69 Gor, 27 May 2019. **Section 6** (definition of personal data), **Section 26** (sensitive personal data categories and prohibition on collection). Supervised by the Personal Data Protection Committee (PDPC) under the Ministry of Digital Economy and Society (MDES).
- **Definition (Section 6 – paraphrased):** “Personal Data” means any information relating to a person, which enables the identification of such person, whether directly or indirectly, but not including the information of deceased persons in particular. Examples include name, address, phone number, identification number, age, gender, IP address, username, and password.
- **Sensitive personal data (Section 26 – exhaustive list):** A data controller shall not collect personal data pertaining to: (1) **racial or ethnic origin**, (2) **political opinions**, (3) **cult, religious, or philosophical beliefs**, (4) **sexual behaviour**, (5) **criminal records**, (6) **health data**, (7) **disability**, (8) **trade union information**, (9) **genetic data**, (10) **biometric data** (defined as data arising from the use of techniques or technology related to the physical or behavioural characteristics of a person enabling identification, such as facial recognition, iris recognition, or fingerprints), or (11) any other data which may affect the data subject in the same manner, as prescribed by the Committee — without explicit consent, subject to exceptions in Section 26 paragraphs 2–7.

## pdpa_sg – Personal Data Protection Act 2012 (Singapore)

- **Legal basis:** Personal Data Protection Act 2012 (No. 26 of 2012), as amended by the Personal Data Protection (Amendment) Act 2020. **Section 2(1)** (definition of personal data). The Act does **not** define a statutory category of “sensitive personal data.” Supervised by the Personal Data Protection Commission (PDPC). PDPC Advisory Guidelines on Key Concepts in the PDPA and Advisory Guidelines on the PDPA for NRIC and Other National Identification Numbers provide supplementary interpretation.
- **Definition (Section 2(1) – paraphrased):** “Personal data” means data, whether true or not, about an individual who can be identified from that data, or from that data and other information to which the organisation has or is likely to have access. The definition covers both electronic and non-electronic data.
- **Sensitive/special categories:** The PDPA does **not** prescribe fixed statutory categories of sensitive personal data. However, PDPC enforcement decisions and advisory guidelines recognise that certain types of personal data are **inherently more sensitive** and require a higher standard of protection, including: NRIC numbers and other national identification numbers, medical/health data, financial data (bank accounts, credit cards), biometric data, criminal records, and data of children. The PDPC's Advisory Guidelines on NRIC Numbers (2018, updated 2020) specifically restrict the collection, use, or disclosure of NRIC numbers, birth certificate numbers, FIN (Foreign Identification Numbers), and work permit numbers.
- **Entity-type mapping:** Name/identification → `PERSON_NAME`, `FIRST_NAME`, `LAST_NAME`, `NATIONAL_ID`, `PASSPORT_NUMBER`; contact → `EMAIL_ADDRESS`, `PHONE_NUMBER`; address/location → `POSTAL_ADDRESS`, `STREET_ADDRESS`, `CITY`, `LOCATION`; dates → `DATE_OF_BIRTH`; online identifiers → `IP_ADDRESS`, `COOKIE_ID`, `DEVICE_ID`; financial → `FINANCIAL_ACCOUNT`, `CREDIT_CARD_NUMBER`, `BANK_ACCOUNT_NUMBER`; health → `HEALTH_INSURANCE_ID`, `DIAGNOSIS`, `MEDICATION`; biometric → `BIOMETRIC_ID`; other identifiers → `EMPLOYEE_ID`.

**References:** [PDPA 2012 (Singapore Statutes Online)](https://sso.agc.gov.sg/Act/PDPA2012), [PDPC Overview of PDPA](https://www.pdpc.gov.sg/overview-of-pdpa/the-legislation/personal-data-protection-act), [PDPC Advisory Guidelines on NRIC Numbers](https://www.pdpc.gov.sg/guidelines-and-consultation/2020/02/advisory-guidelines-on-the-personal-data-protection-act-for-nric-and-other-national-identification-numbers), [IAPP: Singapore PDPA Framework](https://iapp.org/news/a/the-personal-data-protection-framework-in-singapore), [DLA Piper: Singapore](https://www.dlapiperdataprotection.com/?t=law&c=SG).

## appi – Act on the Protection of Personal Information (Japan)

- **Legal basis:** Act on the Protection of Personal Information (Act No. 57 of 2003, as amended 2015, 2020, and 2022; consolidated text effective 1 April 2023). **Article 2(1)** (definition of personal information), **Article 2(2)** (personal identification code), **Article 2(3)** (special care-required personal information). The Cabinet Order to Enforce the Act (Enforcement Order) expands the categories of special care-required personal information. Supervised by the Personal Information Protection Commission (PPC).
- **Definition (Art. 2(1) – paraphrased):** “Personal information” means information relating to a living individual which falls under either of the following: (i) information containing a name, date of birth, or other description by which a specific individual can be identified (including information that can be easily collated with other information and thereby used to identify a specific individual); (ii) information containing a personal identification code (e.g. fingerprint data, iris data, DNA sequence data, passport number, driver's licence number, My Number/individual identification number).
- **Special care-required personal information (Art. 2(3) + Cabinet Order):** Personal information concerning an identifiable person's: (1) **race**, (2) **creed** (religious/philosophical beliefs), (3) **social status**, (4) **medical history**, (5) **criminal record**, (6) **the fact of having suffered damage by a crime**. The Cabinet Order (Enforcement Order) adds: (7) **physical disability, intellectual disability, or mental disability** (including developmental disabilities), (8) **results of medical examinations** or other examinations for disease prevention/early detection, (9) **medical care, guidance, or prescriptions** given by a physician, and (10) information relating to **criminal proceedings** (arrest, investigation, detention). All categories require special care so as not to cause unjust discrimination, prejudice, or other disadvantages.
- **Entity-type mapping:** Name/identification → `PERSON_NAME`, `FIRST_NAME`, `LAST_NAME`, `NATIONAL_ID`, `PASSPORT_NUMBER`; personal identification code → `BIOMETRIC_ID`, `DNA_PROFILE`; contact → `EMAIL_ADDRESS`, `PHONE_NUMBER`; address/location → `POSTAL_ADDRESS`, `STREET_ADDRESS`, `CITY`, `LOCATION`; dates → `DATE_OF_BIRTH`; financial → `FINANCIAL_ACCOUNT`, `CREDIT_CARD_NUMBER`, `BANK_ACCOUNT_NUMBER`; health/medical → `HEALTH_INSURANCE_ID`, `DIAGNOSIS`, `MEDICATION`, `CLINICAL_NOTE`; special care-required → `ETHNICITY`, `RELIGION`, `POLITICAL_OPINION`.

## pipl – Personal Information Protection Law (China)

- **Legal basis:** Personal Information Protection Law of the People's Republic of China, adopted 20 August 2021, effective 1 November 2021. **Article 4** (definition of personal information), **Article 28** (definition and processing rules for sensitive personal information), **Articles 29–32** (additional sensitive PI rules including minors). Supervised by the Cyberspace Administration of China (CAC). Supplemented by recommended national standard GB/T 35273-2020 (Information Security Technology — Personal Information Security Specification).
- **Definition (Art. 4 – paraphrased):** “Personal information” is all kinds of information, recorded by electronic or other means, related to identified or identifiable natural persons, not including information after anonymization handling. Personal information handling includes collection, storage, use, processing, transmission, provision, disclosure, deletion, etc.
- **Sensitive personal information (Art. 28 – paraphrased):** Sensitive personal information means personal information that, once leaked or illegally used, may easily lead to the infringement of the dignity of a natural person or grave harm to personal or property security, including information on: (1) **biometric characteristics**, (2) **religious beliefs**, (3) **specially-designated status** (interpreted to include race, ethnic group, sexual orientation, and specific social identities), (4) **medical health**, (5) **financial accounts**, (6) **individual location tracking**, and (7) **personal information of minors under the age of 14**. Processing requires a specific purpose, necessity, strict protection measures, and separate consent from the individual.
- **Entity-type mapping:** Identification → `PERSON_NAME`, `FIRST_NAME`, `LAST_NAME`, `NATIONAL_ID`, `PASSPORT_NUMBER`; contact → `EMAIL_ADDRESS`, `PHONE_NUMBER`; address/location → `POSTAL_ADDRESS`, `STREET_ADDRESS`, `CITY`, `LOCATION`, `COORDINATES`; dates → `DATE_OF_BIRTH`; online identifiers → `IP_ADDRESS`, `COOKIE_ID`, `DEVICE_ID`; financial → `FINANCIAL_ACCOUNT`, `CREDIT_CARD_NUMBER`, `BANK_ACCOUNT_NUMBER`, `IBAN`; health/medical → `HEALTH_INSURANCE_ID`, `DIAGNOSIS`, `MEDICATION`, `CLINICAL_NOTE`; biometric/genetic → `BIOMETRIC_ID`, `DNA_PROFILE`; sensitive → `RELIGION`, `ETHNICITY`, `POLITICAL_OPINION`, `SEXUAL_ORIENTATION`.

## popia – Protection of Personal Information Act 4 of 2013 (South Africa)

- **Legal basis:** Protection of Personal Information Act 4 of 2013 (POPIA), assented to 19 November 2013, effective 1 July 2020 (with a one-year grace period ending 1 July 2021). **Section 1** (definition of personal information, categories (a)–(h)), **Section 26** (prohibition on processing special personal information), **Sections 27–33** (exceptions for each special category), **Section 35** (processing of personal information of children). Supervised by the Information Regulator.
- **Definition (Section 1 – paraphrased):** “Personal information” means information relating to an identifiable, living, natural person, and where applicable, an identifiable, existing juristic person, including but not limited to: (a) information relating to the **race, gender, sex, pregnancy, marital status, national, ethnic or social origin, colour, sexual orientation, age, physical or mental health, well-being, disability, religion, conscience, belief, culture, language, and birth** of the person; (b) information relating to the **education or the medical, financial, criminal, or employment history** of the person; (c) any **identifying number, symbol, e-mail address, physical address, telephone number, location information, online identifier, or other particular assignment** to the person; (d) the **biometric information** of the person; (e) the **personal opinions, views, or preferences** of the person; (f) **correspondence** sent by the person that is implicitly or explicitly of a private or confidential nature; (g) the **views or opinions of another individual** about the person; (h) the **name** of the person if it appears with other personal information relating to the person or if the disclosure of the name itself would reveal information about the person.

## dpdp – Digital Personal Data Protection Act 2023 (India)

- **Legal basis:** Digital Personal Data Protection Act, 2023 (No. 22 of 2023), enacted 11 August 2023. DPDP Rules 2025 (draft published January 2025). **Section 2(t)** (definition of personal data), **Section 2(n)** (definition of digital personal data), **Section 2(h)** (definition of data), **Section 9** (processing of personal data of children). The Act does **not** define a separate category of “sensitive personal data” — all personal data receives uniform protection. Supervised by the Data Protection Board of India (DPBI), established under the Ministry of Electronics and Information Technology (MeitY).
- **Definition (Section 2(t) – paraphrased):** “Personal data” means any data about an individual who is identifiable by or in relation to such data. “Digital personal data” (Section 2(n)) means personal data in digital form. The Act applies to the processing of digital personal data within India where such data is collected in digital form or collected in non-digital form and subsequently digitised.
- **Sensitive/special categories:** The DPDP Act 2023 does **not** distinguish between personal data and sensitive personal data. This is a deliberate departure from the earlier Personal Data Protection Bill (2019), which had defined sensitive personal data categories (financial data, health data, biometric data, genetic data, caste/tribe, religious/political belief, sexual orientation). Under the 2023 Act, all personal data receives the same level of protection. However, **Section 9** imposes additional obligations for processing personal data of **children** (individuals under 18): verifiable parental/guardian consent is required, and behavioural monitoring, tracking, and targeted advertising directed at children are prohibited. The Central Government may designate certain Data Fiduciaries as **Significant Data Fiduciaries** (Section 10) based on volume and sensitivity of data processed, imposing additional obligations (DPO appointment, data audit, DPIA).
- **Entity-type mapping:** Name/identification → `PERSON_NAME`, `FIRST_NAME`, `LAST_NAME`, `NATIONAL_ID`, `PASSPORT_NUMBER`, `TAX_ID`; contact → `EMAIL_ADDRESS`, `PHONE_NUMBER`; address/location → `POSTAL_ADDRESS`, `STREET_ADDRESS`, `CITY`, `LOCATION`, `COORDINATES`; dates → `DATE_OF_BIRTH`; online identifiers → `IP_ADDRESS`, `COOKIE_ID`, `DEVICE_ID`; financial → `FINANCIAL_ACCOUNT`, `CREDIT_CARD_NUMBER`, `BANK_ACCOUNT_NUMBER`; health → `HEALTH_INSURANCE_ID`, `DIAGNOSIS`, `MEDICATION`; biometric → `BIOMETRIC_ID`.

## How this affects the product

- **Policy composer** builds the active entity set from all active regulation rulesets (union of their `entity_types`).
- **Sanitizer** (Presidio, NER, optional Ollama layer) is asked to detect only those entity types, so masking is aligned with the regulations the user has enabled.
- **Generic labels** (e.g. the word for “address” in a language) must not be hardcoded; they can be excluded from masking via the **NonPiiRule** table (Settings/API) if desired.
